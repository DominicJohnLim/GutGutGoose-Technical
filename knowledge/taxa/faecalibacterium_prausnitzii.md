# Faecalibacterium prausnitzii
- key: taxa:faecalibacterium_prausnitzii
- what it is: one of the most abundant bacteria in a healthy adult gut; a standout beneficial commensal
- does: a leading producer of butyrate — the main fuel for the cells lining the colon, and an anti-inflammatory signal
- associations: low levels are associated with inflammatory bowel disease and general gut inflammation (association, NOT proof). It is often described as a marker of a healthy gut.
- typical healthy range: ~3-15% in healthy adults
- confidence: well-established
- citation: Sokol et al., PNAS 2008 (anti-inflammatory role); Lopez-Siles et al., ISME J 2017 (review) [verify in human-gate]
