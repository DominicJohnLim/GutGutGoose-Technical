# Thomasclavelia ramosa (formerly Clostridium ramosum)
- key: taxa:thomasclavelia_ramosa
- what it is: a common gut commensal, reclassified over the years (Clostridium ramosum -> Erysipelatoclostridium -> Thomasclavelia ramosa)
- does: ferments carbohydrates; an ordinary member of the normal gut community
- associations: in mouse studies, higher levels have been associated with increased dietary fat absorption and weight gain (animal data, NOT proof in people); occasionally an opportunist in clinical settings
- typical healthy range: usually low to moderate
- confidence: moderate (the diet/weight link is from animal models)
- citation: Woting et al., mBio 2014 (C. ramosum and fat absorption in mice) [verify in human-gate]
