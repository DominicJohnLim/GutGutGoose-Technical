# Veillonella (e.g. Veillonella ratti)
- key: taxa:veillonella
- what it is: a small anaerobic gut bacterium with an unusual diet
- does: does NOT eat sugars; instead it specializes in consuming lactate (made by other microbes) and converting it into propionate and acetate — a classic "cross-feeder" that tidies up other microbes' byproducts
- associations: a normal gut resident; levels partly reflect how much lactate is around. Some studies link Veillonella to lactate metabolism during exercise (association, not proof).
- typical healthy range: usually a minor member (under ~1-2%). Notably higher levels are worth a gentle look, as they can reflect a lactate-rich community.
- confidence: established (lactate cross-feeding metabolism)
