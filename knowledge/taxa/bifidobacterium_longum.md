# Bifidobacterium longum
- key: taxa:bifidobacterium_longum
- what it is: a classic "good" gut bacterium, widely used in probiotics; abundant in infancy and persists in adults
- does: ferments dietary fibers (and milk oligosaccharides) into lactate and acetate; helps support the gut barrier and crowd out less friendly microbes
- associations: generally considered beneficial; commonly supplemented as a probiotic (association, not proof of benefit in any individual)
- typical healthy range: the Bifidobacterium genus is often ~1-10% in healthy adults
- confidence: well-established
- citation: Turroni et al., reviews on Bifidobacterium in the gut [verify in human-gate]
