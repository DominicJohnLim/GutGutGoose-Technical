# Clostridium innocuum
- key: taxa:clostridium_innocuum
- what it is: a common gut commensal found in many healthy people
- does: ferments carbohydrates as part of the normal community
- associations: usually harmless; in some clinical settings (especially after antibiotics) it can act as an opportunist. At ordinary background levels it is unremarkable. (association with antibiotic disruption, not proof)
- typical healthy range: low
- confidence: moderate
- citation: general gut-commensal literature [verify in human-gate]
