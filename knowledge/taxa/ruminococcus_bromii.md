# Ruminococcus bromii
- key: taxa:ruminococcus_bromii
- what it is: common gut commensal; a recognized "keystone" species for fiber breakdown
- does: the primary degrader of resistant starch (from cooled potatoes/rice, legumes, whole grains). It cracks open starch that the body can't digest so other microbes can ferment it into short-chain fatty acids.
- associations: higher with whole-grain / high-fiber diets; as a keystone, its presence lets the whole community use resistant starch (association, not proof)
- typical healthy range: varies widely, ~1-13%; can be a dominant member
- confidence: well-established (keystone role)
- citation: Ze et al., ISME J 2012 — R. bromii as a keystone for resistant-starch degradation [verify in human-gate]
