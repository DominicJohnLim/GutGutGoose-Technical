# Bacteroides / Phocaeicola / Parabacteroides (the Bacteroides group)
- key: taxa:bacteroides
- what it is: a group of dominant gut "generalists" (includes Bacteroides stercoris, B. fragilis, B. thetaiotaomicron, Phocaeicola dorei, Parabacteroides distasonis). Very common backbone of the adult gut.
- does: extremely versatile — break down a wide range of dietary fibers AND proteins, producing propionate and acetate. Flexible eaters that adapt to whatever the diet provides.
- associations: core, normal gut bacteria. Diets higher in animal protein/fat tend to favor this group; fiber-rich diets favor others. B. fragilis is a helpful gut resident but an opportunist if it escapes the gut. (associations, not proof)
- typical healthy range: collectively often a large share of the community (~10-40%)
- confidence: well-established
- citation: Human Microbiome Project, Nature 2012; Wexler, Clin Microbiol Rev 2007 (Bacteroides review) [verify in human-gate]
