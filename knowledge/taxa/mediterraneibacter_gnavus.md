# Mediterraneibacter gnavus (formerly Ruminococcus gnavus)
- key: taxa:mediterraneibacter_gnavus
- what it is: a common gut bacterium (recently reclassified out of Ruminococcus). Present at low levels in many healthy people.
- does: can degrade mucin (the gut's protective mucus layer) and, when it blooms, makes an inflammatory sugar molecule
- associations: blooms (large increases) are associated with inflammatory bowel disease flares and with some inflammatory/allergic conditions (association, NOT proof). At low background levels it is unremarkable.
- typical healthy range: usually low (under ~2%)
- confidence: established association at high abundance
- citation: Hall et al., Genome Medicine 2017 (R. gnavus blooms in IBD) [verify in human-gate]
