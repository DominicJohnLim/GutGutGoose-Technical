# Concept: short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), esp. butyrate
- key: concepts:scfa
- what it is: small molecules (butyrate, propionate, acetate) that gut bacteria make when they ferment dietary fiber
- why they matter: butyrate is the main fuel for the cells lining the colon and has anti-inflammatory effects; propionate and acetate feed into whole-body metabolism. They are a big part of "why fiber is good for you."
- who makes them: butyrate producers include Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, Roseburia, Fusicatenibacter, Anaerostipes; cross-feeders like Veillonella convert lactate into propionate/acetate; resistant-starch degraders like Ruminococcus bromii feed the whole chain
- how to talk about it: frame SCFA-producing bacteria as a positive, tied to a fiber-rich diet, without over-claiming health outcomes for an individual
- confidence: well-established
- citation: Louis & Flint, Environ Microbiol 2017 (formation of butyrate/propionate by the colonic microbiota) [verify in human-gate]
