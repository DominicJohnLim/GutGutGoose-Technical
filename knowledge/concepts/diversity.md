# Concept: diversity (Shannon, richness, evenness)
- key: concepts:diversity
- what it is: summary numbers describing the gut community as a whole, not any single bacterium
  - richness = how many different species are present
  - Shannon index = combines how many species AND how evenly they are spread (one dominant bug = lower; a balanced mix = higher)
  - evenness = how balanced vs. lopsided the mix is
- key nuance: **low diversity is NOT automatically bad.** Context matters. Some healthy people — for example those on certain plant-forward or restricted diets — run lower diversity yet have excellent gut health. Diversity is one lens, not a grade.
- how to talk about it: describe the number plainly, avoid implying a low number means "unhealthy", and lean on what specific bacteria are doing rather than the single diversity score
- confidence: well-established (the metrics); the "low = bad" oversimplification is explicitly cautioned against
- citation: standard ecological diversity measures applied to the microbiome [verify in human-gate]
